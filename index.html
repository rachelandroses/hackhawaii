Who We Are

We're not what our
name suggests

Despite the name, HackHawaii has nothing to do with hacking into systems. We are a safe, welcoming nonprofit where everyday people — beginners and builders alike — learn to use AI and technology confidently and responsibly.

We run community events, speak at schools across the islands, and operate a dedicated mentorship program to help people with disabilities and Native Hawaiians land meaningful careers in tech.

Our work is grounded in the belief that AI and technology should serve everyone — not just those who already have technical backgrounds. Whether you are a curious beginner, a student, an educator, or someone ready to launch a career, HackHawaii is your community.

Mentorship Program

A career in tech
is within reach

HackHawaii runs a dedicated mentorship program for people with disabilities and Native Hawaiians who want to launch or grow a career in technology. We believe talent is everywhere — opportunity should be too.

Our mentors are working professionals who guide you through skill-building, project work, networking, and the job search. Applications are reviewed on a rolling basis.

Who this program is for

  • People with physical, cognitive, or other disabilities
  • Native Hawaiian and Pacific Islander community members
  • Residents of Hawaiʻi — no prior tech experience required
  • Motivated individuals committed to learning and growing

Apply for Mentorship

We review applications on a rolling basis and respond within one week.

Our application form is hosted on Google Forms. It takes about 5 minutes to complete and asks about your background, goals, and what you hope to gain from the program.
