About the Program

Leadership starts here

HackHawaii Girls is a free summer leadership program designed to help young women in Hawaiʻi discover their strengths, challenge stereotypes, and build the confidence to go after anything they want.

We partner with Lean In Girls — a program created by Sheryl Sandberg's Lean In Foundation — to deliver a structured, research-backed curriculum that has empowered thousands of girls worldwide.

Our program is facilitated by a female tech leader with experience at Google, bringing real-world perspective on what it means to build a career, lead teams, and break barriers in one of the world's most competitive industries.

Sessions are held every Saturday morning — in person at Ala Moana on Oʻahu or live on Zoom — so girls from across the island can participate.

As a special bonus woven throughout the program, girls will also learn how to use AI tools safely and responsibly — practical skills that will serve them in school, future careers, and everyday life.

Lean In Girls
A program by the Lean In Foundation

Lean In Girls is a free curriculum designed to help girls ages 8–18 develop confidence, build leadership skills, and learn to advocate for themselves. It was created in partnership with world-class researchers and educators.

The curriculum covers values, strengths, resilience, risk-taking, goal-setting, and self-advocacy — everything girls need to thrive in school, work, and life.

Learn more at leanin.org/girls

What a session looks like

Welcome circle (10 min) — Quick check-in, celebrations from the week, set intentions for today
Lesson & discussion (35 min) — Dive into the week's topic with stories, research, and open conversation
Activity & reflection (30 min) — Hands-on exercises, journaling, and group work to make it real
Share backs (10 min) — Each girl shares one insight or commitment from the session
Closing & homework (5 min) — A small weekly challenge to practice leadership in daily life
